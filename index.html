Known as one of the last great soul singers, Al Green has reigned as one of the most influential musicians of the millienium. Coming from a deeply devoted Christian home, he was kicked out of his house by his father for listening to Jackie Wilson, a famous soul singer during Green's adolescence. His first significant single was "So Tired of Being Alone" off his album "Gets Next to You." One of his most widely recognized hits is "Lets Stay Together." Aside from soul, he has released gospel projects, including "The Lord Will May" in 1980. He became an ordained pastor in 1976. Green was inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame in 1995.
